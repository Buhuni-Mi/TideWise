Feedback Page

Technical Description

My feedback page combines three areas: the programme directory, the reviews section, and the feedback form. I kept these sections visually separate so the page is easier to read. The form uses JavaScript for the live character count, error handling, and the final success message.

I used `fieldset` and `legend` to group form details clearly. I also added a scrollable programme area and review cards so the page meets the layout requirements without becoming cluttered.

Accessibility

The form fields have clear labels, the page sections are separated by headings, and the review text is kept readable rather than tightly packed. Error feedback is also tied to the form instead of being hidden in styling alone.

Team Page

Technical Description

The team page introduces all four group members and shows their responsibilities. I used a card layout and simple show-and-hide details so users can quickly see who worked on which part of the site.

The page uses hover and keyboard-friendly interaction rather than advanced animation. That made it easier to keep the design simple and also easier to explain.

Accessibility

The team cards can be focused by keyboard, the member photos use alt text, and the buttons use clear labels instead of relying on hover alone. I kept the text on each card short so the information is easy to scan.

Content Page

Technical Description

My content page focuses on community programmes that support marine protection. I wrote about volunteering, awareness events, and local partnerships because those ideas match the action-based purpose of the coursework.

The page uses internal links, section headings, and supporting images in the same style as the other content pages so the whole site stays visually consistent.

Accessibility

I used clear section headings, meaningful image alt text, and internal contents links so the page is easier to move through. The content is also broken into manageable sections instead of long paragraphs.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

The feedback page was more difficult than I expected because it combines content, layout, and form validation on one page. I also had to fix small text issues and keep the form feedback clear but not too aggressive. Breaking the page into clear sections made it easier to manage.

Accessibility Considerations

For my pages, accessibility mostly came down to readability and interaction feedback. The form fields have clear labels, the team cards can be focused by keyboard, and text sections are broken into smaller readable parts instead of long blocks. I also made sure the review cards and programme cards are visually separate, which helps users follow the page more easily.

Compliance

My pages follow JANET expectations by keeping the language respectful, suitable for academic use, and relevant to the SDG theme. I avoided inappropriate user-generated features, kept the reviews clearly sample-based rather than misleading, and used content that fits a university coursework website instead of copying promotional material from other sites.
